SPONSOR POLICY (NSF / NIH / DoD STYLE)

(Must follow Federal AND sponsor requirements.)

1. Personnel

Allowed:
- PI and Co-PI effort as listed in the approved proposal.
- Graduate students, undergraduates, and technicians budgeted in proposal.
- Salary changes that receive sponsor approval.
- Personnel expenses under $12,000 per person per year.
- Personnel expenses > $12,000 allowed with prior approval.

Not Allowed:
- Personnel not listed in the proposal without sponsor approval.
- Administrative staff unless pre-approved and justified.
- Charging more effort than proposed without approval.

Rules:
- Reducing PI effort by >25% requires sponsor approval.
- NIH and NSF salary caps must be followed.
- Personnel expenses exceeding $12,000 per person per year require prior approval.

Violations:
- Adding a new technician without sponsor notification.
- Hiding administrative staff charges inside "other direct costs."

2. Equipment

Allowed:
- Equipment explicitly listed in the proposal budget and justification.
- Scientific upgrades or replacements with valid justification.
- Equipment under $8,000 per item.
- Equipment > $8,000 allowed with prior approval.

Not Allowed:
- Equipment not originally included in the proposal.
- General-purpose equipment unless justified.

Rules:
- Equipment exceeding $8,000 per item requires prior approval.

Violations:
- Buying drones/robots not listed in proposal.
- Purchasing extra laptops "for convenience."

3. Travel (Sponsor-Specific)

Allowed:
- Fieldwork travel, conferences, required sponsor meetings.
- International travel allowed if:
  - Fly America Act is followed AND
  - It benefits the project.
- Travel expenses under $5,000 per trip.
- Travel expenses > $5,000 allowed with prior approval.

Not Allowed:
- Personal or recreational travel.
- Travel for individuals not working on the project.

Sponsor Rules:
- NSF requires travel justification tied to project outcomes.
- DoD may require pre-trip security briefings.
- Travel expenses exceeding $5,000 per trip require prior approval.

Violations:
- Charging flight costs for family members.
- Attending unrelated conferences.

4. Materials & Supplies

Allowed:
- Research materials, chemicals, reagents, consumables necessary for project.
- Materials and supplies under $5,000 per item.
- Materials and supplies > $5,000 allowed with prior approval.

Not Allowed:
- Office supplies not directly linked to research.
- Personal items or non-research consumables.

Rules:
- Materials and supplies exceeding $5,000 per item require prior approval.

5. Other Direct Costs

Allowed:
- Participant incentives, publication fees, specialized services.
- Other direct costs under $5,000 per item.
- Other direct costs > $5,000 allowed with prior approval.

Not Allowed:
- Unrelated consulting or services.
- Membership dues unless required for publishing.

Rules:
- Other direct costs exceeding $5,000 per item require prior approval.

6. Participant Support Costs (PSC)

Allowed:
- Stipends, lodging, meals for participants.
- Travel only for participants.

Not Allowed:
- PI or administrative salary from PSC funds.
- Equipment purchases.

Violations:
- Buying laptops from PSC funds.
- Charging PI time to PSC.

7. Subawards

Allowed:
- Subrecipients named in the proposal.
- Adding new subrecipients with sponsor modification.

Not Allowed:
- Paying a consultant as a subawardee without contract.

Violations:
- Paying partners without updated award documents.
- No monitoring reports from subrecipients.
