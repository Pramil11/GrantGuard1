UNIVERSITY POLICY

(Internal policy that must follow Federal + Sponsor requirements.)

1. Personnel (Salary & Wages)

Allowed:
- Individuals directly contributing to project aims.
- Graduate assistants under HR rules.
- Undergraduate researchers with defined tasks.
- Personnel expenses under $12,000 per person per year.
- Personnel expenses > $12,000 allowed with prior approval.

Not Allowed:
- Administrative, clerical, HR, IT staff unless 100% allocated to project.
- Paying before work is performed.
- Charging salary during breaks or leave without effort worked.

Rules:
- Must follow institutional base salary rates.
- Hourly workers require timesheets.
- Personnel expenses exceeding $12,000 per person per year require prior approval.

Violations:
- Charging a secretary for occasional help.
- Charging salary during vacation or sick leave.

2. Equipment

Allowed:
- Research equipment under university threshold (e.g., < $8,000).
- Equipment > $8,000 allowed with prior approval.
- Repairs and maintenance.

Not Allowed:
- Personal electronics (AirPods, iPads for personal use).
- Office furniture unless related to specialized equipment.

Violations:
- Buying a TV for a research lounge.
- Purchasing GoPro cameras without a research purpose.

3. Travel

Allowed:
- Domestic and international travel that supports research.
- Conference travel where PI or students present project work.
- University-approved per-diem rates.
- Travel expenses under $5,000 per trip.
- Travel expenses > $5,000 allowed with prior approval.

Not Allowed:
- First-class or business-class travel.
- Personal vacation time charged to the grant.
- Alcohol.

Rules:
- Travel expenses exceeding $5,000 per trip require prior approval.

Violations:
- Charging a hotel for extra personal days.
- Buying alcoholic beverages.

4. Materials & Supplies

Allowed:
- Chemicals, reagents, consumables.
- Project-specific software licenses.
- Research computing supplies.
- Materials and supplies under $5,000 per item.
- Materials and supplies > $5,000 allowed with prior approval.

Not Allowed:
- Office supplies not directly linked to research.
- Food/snacks/coffee.
- Decorative items.

Rules:
- Materials and supplies exceeding $5,000 per item require prior approval.

Violations:
- Buying balloons for lab events.
- Printer ink for general use.

5. Other Direct Costs

Allowed:
- Participant incentives.
- Publication and open-access fees.
- Specialized data storage fees.
- Other direct costs under $5,000 per item.
- Other direct costs > $5,000 allowed with prior approval.

Not Allowed:
- Membership dues unless required for publishing.
- Consulting unrelated to research.

Rules:
- Other direct costs exceeding $5,000 per item require prior approval.

Violations:
- Paying honoraria not budgeted.
- Charging unrelated software subscriptions.

POLICY HIERARCHY (LLM MUST FOLLOW THIS):
1. Federal (highest priority) - Reject if federal policy is violated.
2. Sponsor (middle priority) - Reject if sponsor policy violates federal or sponsor rules.
3. University (lowest priority) - Reject if university prohibits something not prohibited above.

LLM should evaluate in this exact sequence.
