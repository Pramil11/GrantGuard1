FEDERAL POLICY 
(Highest authority. All sponsored projects MUST follow these rules.)

1. Personnel & Salary Charges

Allowed:
- Salary proportional to the percentage of effort actually worked.
- Compensation must match institutional base salary and pay rates.
- Graduate assistants, postdocs, and technicians performing research tasks.
- Administrative/clerical salary only when specifically justified and approved.
- Personnel expenses under $12,000 per person per year.
- Personnel expenses > $12,000 allowed with prior approval.

Not Allowed:
- Charging salary for effort not actually worked.
- Increasing pay because grant money is available.
- Charging two grants for the same effort period.
- Charging voluntary uncommitted effort later to the grant.

Rules:
- Effort = salary percentage and must match certified effort reports.
- Personnel charges must satisfy "allowable, allocable, reasonable, consistently treated."
- Personnel expenses exceeding $12,000 per person per year require prior approval.

Violations:
- Charging 30% salary for someone who worked 5%.
- PI exceeding 100% effort across multiple grants.
- Retroactively raising salary to use remaining funds.

2. Equipment

Allowed:
- Research equipment necessary to complete project aims.
- Equipment purchased through competitive procurement.
- Scientific equipment repairs, calibration, and maintenance.

Not Allowed:
- Splitting purchases to avoid procurement thresholds.
- General-purpose equipment (office chairs, standard laptops) without justification.
- "Use-it-or-lose-it" purchases at the end of the grant.

Rules:
- $8,000+ requires formal procurement or sole-source justification.
- Federal title, tracking, and disposition rules apply.

Violations:
- Two $4,000 purchases to avoid the $8k procurement rule.
- Buying a general-use iMac for PI office use.

3. Travel

Allowed:
- Economy-class travel related to the research project.
- International travel following the Fly America Act.
- Per diem rates must follow federal guidelines.
- Travel expenses under $5,000 per trip.
- Travel expenses > $5,000 allowed with prior approval.

Not Allowed:
- Business/first-class travel (except disability accommodation).
- Charging vacation time as grant travel.
- Using non-U.S. carriers without Fly America exception.

Rules:
- Travel must clearly benefit the project scientifically.
- Fly America exceptions must be documented before reimbursement.
- Travel expenses exceeding $5,000 per trip require prior approval.

Violations:
- Booking a foreign airline without exemption documentation.
- Charging weekend hotel stays unrelated to research.

4. Subawards & Procurement

Allowed:
- Competitive bidding for goods and services.
- Formal written subaward agreements with monitoring.
- Sole-source procurement only with strong justification.

Not Allowed:
- Paying individuals without contracts.
- Conflicts of interest (family vendors, personal companies).
- Splitting procurement into smaller amounts to avoid thresholds.

Rules:
- All vendors must pass conflict-of-interest checks.
- Subrecipients must follow Uniform Guidance.

Violations:
- Hiring PI's cousin's business.
- Missing quotes or missing monitoring documents.

5. Materials & Supplies

Allowed:
- Research materials, chemicals, reagents, consumables necessary for project.
- Materials and supplies under $5,000 per item.
- Materials and supplies > $5,000 allowed with prior approval.

Not Allowed:
- Office supplies not directly linked to research.
- Personal items or non-research consumables.

Rules:
- Materials and supplies exceeding $5,000 per item require prior approval.

6. Other Direct Costs

Allowed:
- Participant incentives, publication fees, specialized services.
- Other direct costs under $5,000 per item.
- Other direct costs > $5,000 allowed with prior approval.

Not Allowed:
- Unrelated consulting or services.
- Membership dues unless required for publishing.

Rules:
- Other direct costs exceeding $5,000 per item require prior approval.
